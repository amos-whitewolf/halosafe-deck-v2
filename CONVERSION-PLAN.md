## TL;DR — what's broken, what to ship first

The deck is a **5/10 brand piece, 3/10 conversion page.** The chrome, typography and rhythm are excellent (and a moat). The copy is mission-statement-led and assumes a Product-Aware audience that doesn't exist in cold paid traffic.

**Three fixes do 70% of the lift, in order:**

1. **Hero rewrite** — kill the "Quietly." mission tagline as primary; lead with the loss-aversion hook ("The conversations your kid won't tell you about. Halo hears them.") with risk-reversal CTA microcopy ("No card. 14-day trial.")
2. **Insert the "blind spot" comparison slide** — Bark watches text, Qustodio watches apps, Screen Time counts hours. None listen to voice chat. This is the bridge from Solution-Aware → Halo-Aware that the deck currently skips.
3. **Fix three lying details** — pricing ($9.99/mo → real $96/yr), iOS status (TestFlight → App Store), Discord (Soon → real status). Anything cold traffic can verify must be true.

After that: testimonial slide, comparison table, pricing anchor + risk reversal promotion, final CTA tighten.

The moat — Anatomy of a Flag, Voice/Tone yes/no, the 5 Privacy rules, deck-slide chrome, editorial typography — **does not get touched.** Those are why the deck is taken seriously.

## 1. Schwartz Awareness Stage — where Sarah is, what we can say

**Stage:** mostly **Solution-Aware**, with a Problem-Aware tail.

- ✓ Knows voice chat is risky.
- ✓ Knows generic parental controls exist (Bark, Qustodio, Screen Time).
- ✗ Does NOT know on-device voice AI exists.
- ✗ Does NOT know Halo (not Product-Aware).

**Messaging rules (do):**
- Compare against what she's already considered. ("Bark watches text. Halo listens to voice.")
- Educate on the voice-chat blind spot before pitching.
- Use loss aversion — what's happening *right now* that she can't hear.
- Foreground risk reversal — free trial, no card, cancel any time.

**Messaging rules (don't):**
- Don't sell features to a Product-Aware audience that doesn't exist yet. (Current `01 · Mission` essay assumes she already cares about Halo specifically.)
- Don't lead with mission, manifesto, or "why we built this." That's slide 4, not slide 1.
- Don't use insider category language ("voice safety platform"). She doesn't know the category.

**Angle direction:** **Comparison + Education** (with Agitation as a hook layer).

## 2. Mechanism — why Halo works when others didn't

**Failed method (what Sarah's tried or seen):**
- Bark / Qustodio / Apple Screen Time → watch text and apps. Voice chat is invisible to all of them.
- "Just talk to your kid" → her kid won't repeat what was said.
- Server-side recording tools → kids find the icon, parents drown in alerts, privacy is a nightmare.

**Why it didn't work:**
- Text monitoring misses 100% of voice chat — and voice is where the worst conversations happen.
- Cloud-recording invades the kid's space and trains them to play silent.
- Always-on alerts train parents to ignore them.

**New mechanism: Quiet Listening™**
- Audio is processed **on the device, in real time.**
- Nothing leaves the phone unless a real flag fires.
- The parent only hears from Halo when it matters.

**Three pay-off lines (use as proof rails throughout the page):**
1. **Where the harm happens** — voice chat, not text.
2. **On the phone, not in the cloud** — privacy is architecture, not policy.
3. **Silent until it matters** — you only hear from us when you need to.

**Name candidates** (pick one and own it):
- **Quiet Listening™** ← recommended (matches existing "Quietly", "All quiet" voice)
- On-Device Voice Watch
- Silent Backstop

## 4. Objection Crusher — 8 objections × 3 reframes each

Use these as FAQ entries, pricing-card microcopy, and CTA-adjacent reassurance.

### O1 — "$96/year is too much for an app I might not use."
- **Logic:** ~$8/month. Cheaper than one therapy session. Covers every device in the family.
- **Proof:** 14-day free trial. No credit card. Cancel any time, we delete everything within 24 hours.
- **Emotion:** "We charge for the 30 seconds of worry you can stop carrying when your kid sits down to play."

### O2 — "Won't my kid feel spied on?"
- **Logic:** No session recording. No transcript of yesterday. Only flagged moments are kept.
- **Proof:** 5-rule privacy section — voice never leaves the phone, only flagged moments uploaded, no third-party sharing, deleted on uninstall.
- **Emotion:** "Tell your kid Halo is running. Kids who know they have a backstop are safer than kids who think no one's listening."

### O3 — "Won't this just spam me with false alarms?"
- **Logic:** Fewer than 1% of sessions produce a flag. Most days say "all quiet."
- **Proof:** Halo's home screen is the same boring "all quiet" 99% of the time — by design.
- **Emotion:** "A safety tool that buzzes all day teaches you to ignore it. We won't do that to you."

### O4 — "Does it actually work on Roblox / Fortnite / Minecraft?"
- **Logic:** Halo joins voice chat as a passive listener — same way your kid's friends do. Live on all three today.
- **Proof:** Live coverage badges next to each platform. Three platforms account for almost all the harm.
- **Emotion:** "We started where the harm is — not where it was easy to ship."

### O5 — "What if it misses the bad stuff?"
- **Logic:** Trained on grooming language, threats, and personal-info / image asks — the patterns that lead to harm.
- **Proof:** Real example flag on the page. One severity. One transcript line. Two buttons.
- **Emotion:** "We'd rather miss a marginal moment than wake you up at 11pm for nothing. Real flags, not noise."

### O6 — "Is this just another Bark?"
- **Logic:** Bark and Qustodio scan text and apps. None listen to voice chat. Halo is the only one that does.
- **Proof:** Comparison row on the page (Halo vs Bark vs Qustodio vs Screen Time — voice chat: ✓ ✕ ✕ ✕).
- **Emotion:** "Most parental controls were built before voice chat was the thing. Halo was built because it is."

### O7 — "Will my kid find it and turn it off?"
- **Logic:** No icon on the home screen. No sound. No interruption.
- **Proof:** Section 04 rule #5 — invisible to the kid, visible to you.
- **Emotion:** "Halo is a backstop, not a punishment."

### O8 — "What if I install it and forget about it?"
- **Logic:** That's the design. Halo is silent by default. The next push you get is the one that matters.
- **Proof:** "Most of the time, nothing happens. That's the point." (already on page — promote to hero proof line)
- **Emotion:** "The whole point is to give you peace of mind, not another notification to clear."

### Existing FAQ keepers (already strong)
- O9 — Android availability (already in FAQ — keep)
- O10 — Discord / WhatsApp availability (already in FAQ — but **fix the answer to match real status**)
- O11 — Should I tell my child? (already in FAQ — keep, this one earns trust)

### Mental models to deploy

- **Loss aversion** → reframe hero from "we're quiet" to "what's happening that you can't hear"
- **Hyperbolic discounting** → move "Free for 14 days" from a footnote to the *primary* CTA chip (immediate reward)
- **Default effect** → if a monthly plan exists, pre-select annual ($96/yr) at the discount default
- **Paradox of choice** → keep ONE plan only ✓ already correct
- **Pratfall effect** → promote the honest "Not on Android yet, won't ship until it matters" line from FAQ to a visible pull-quote — earns trust through admitted weakness
- **Goal-gradient** → onboarding screens should show "Step 2 of 3 — almost there"
- **Peak-end rule** → thank-you / install-confirmation screen should restate the deal: "You'll only hear from us when it matters. Until then, enjoy the quiet."

### Levers PRESENT but UNDER-used

- **Commitment & consistency** — free trial is a small commitment ✓, but no soft email-capture for non-buyers (foot-in-the-door is missing)
- **Liking** — founder voice is in the copy tonally ✓, but no founder photo, name, or 2-sentence story
- **Unity** — "Built by parents, for parents" is in the footer — should appear in the **hero or mission**, not buried at the bottom
