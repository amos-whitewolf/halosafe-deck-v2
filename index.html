Mechanism 04 · How it listens

How Halo listens

Halo runs on the device.
Not in the cloud.

Voice is processed on-device, in real time, while your child is playing. Nothing leaves the phone unless something is flagged — and then only the moment that matters.

01
Halo joins the call as a passive listener. It hears voice chat the same way your child's friends do.
02
Audio is transcribed and analyzed on the device, never streamed. Words leave the phone only when a flag is created.
03
When a real risk is detected, you get a single push notification. Severity, what was said, and what to do next.
04
When nothing is happening, Halo says so. "All quiet" is the most common state.
05
Your child can't see Halo. No icon on the home screen. No sound. No interruption.

Anatomy of a flag
severity
One pill, one color. Red is reserved for things that need a parent now.
headline
Plain language. Past tense. What actually happened, not what we detected.
transcript
One line maximum. The exact words, in quotes. Never paraphrased.

Honest answers 13 · FAQ

Honest answers

Questions parents ask.

01 Is $99.99 a year actually worth it? +
Around $8.33 a month. Cheaper than one therapy session if something goes wrong, cheaper than the time you'd spend reconstructing a conversation your kid won't fully repeat. One subscription covers every child in the family across iPhone, Mac and Windows. If after 60 days Halo hasn't shown you anything worth seeing, email us and we'll refund the year.
02 Isn't this the same as Bark or Qustodio? +
No. Bark and Qustodio scan text and apps. Apple Screen Time counts hours. None of them listen to voice chat — and voice is where most of the harmful conversations now happen. Halo is the only tool that hears what's being said in Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft and Discord. Most parents run Halo alongside something like Bark, not instead of it.
03 Won't this just spam me with false alarms? +
Fewer than 1% of sessions produce a flag. The default state of Halo's home screen is "all quiet" — that's by design. A flag only fires when the model is confident a moment matches a known harmful pattern: grooming language, threats, or asks for personal info or images. You can mark any flag as "this was fine" to teach the model your child's context.
04 Does my child know Halo is running? +
No. Halo runs in the background and doesn't appear on the home screen. There's no icon, no sound, and no notification on the child's device. They won't know it's there unless you tell them — and we recommend you do. Kids who know they have a backstop are safer than kids who think no one's listening.
05 Is voice chat actually being recorded? +
No. Halo processes audio on-device, in real time. When a session ends with no flags, nothing is kept. When a flag is created, only the seconds that triggered it are uploaded — never the whole session, never an entire day. Uninstall Halo and we delete everything within 24 hours.
06 What if Halo misses something serious? +
No tool catches everything, and we won't pretend otherwise. Halo is trained on grooming language, threats and asks for personal info or images — the patterns that lead to actual harm. We tune for fewer false alarms, not more. You can review every flag in the app, and the conversation with your kid is the real safety net. Halo is the early warning, not the whole net.
07 Will my kid find it and turn it off? +
Halo is invisible to the kid on iPhone — no icon, no sound, no obvious app to delete. On Mac and Windows it runs in the background like any other system service. Settings sit on your parent device, not theirs. If your kid does manage to disable it, you'll get a notification immediately.
08 Does it work on Roblox? Even after their voice changes? +
Yes. Halo joins voice chat as a passive listener — the same way your kid's friends do. Roblox, Fortnite, Minecraft are all live today. Voice chat in Roblox is restricted to verified users 13+ in some regions, but the in-game audio is still captured wherever your kid uses it.
09 Discord, FaceTime, WhatsApp voice — does it cover those? +
Discord is live on Mac and Windows today. FaceTime, WhatsApp voice and other VoIP apps on iPhone are technically harder — iOS routes those audio streams privately and we won't fake coverage we can't deliver. The mic captures what the child says either way, which is the primary signal. We'll expand platforms when we can do them properly, not before.
10 Does Halo work on Android? +
Not yet. We focused on iPhone, Mac and Windows first because the system audio APIs are more constrained and predictable. Android is on the roadmap but won't ship until we can match the same quality bar. Most families have at least one device Halo runs on today.
11 Will it slow down their games? +
No measurable impact on Mac, Windows, or modern iPhones (iPhone 12 and newer). Halo is a small on-device model running alongside the game, not a video call recorder. We tested with kids on actual hardware before shipping, and frame rate / latency stays unchanged.
12 What happens if I cancel? +
Halo stops listening immediately and we delete every flag, every clip and every account record within 24 hours. No retention period, no "we'll keep this for analytics," no email funnel begging you back. If you want to come back later, you start fresh.
13 Should I tell my child Halo is running? +
Yes. Halo is not a surveillance tool — it's a quiet net. Kids who know there's a backstop are demonstrably safer than kids who don't. The conversation we recommend is short: "If anything weird happens in voice chat, I'll know, and I'm here to help. You're not in trouble for any of it."
